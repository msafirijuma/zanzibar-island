About Zanzibar

Zanzibar is an island in the Indian Ocean 25-50 kilometres off the coast of the mainland,it is composed of Unguja and Pemba and other small islands. Zanzibar is among the largest islands in the Indian Ocean. .In 1964, Zanzibar joined with Tanganyika to form a United Republic of Tanzania.

Among the most interesting facts about Zanzibar is, the island was ruled and colonized by Arabs,Portugueses, and finally British Empire which left alot of historical information, sites, and history which are very useful to study.

Zanzibar is an island of tour and history thus, by visiting this wonderful island will get you to the point of study tour the history of Afican Colonialism, cultures, traditions and other wondeful sites to see such as The Old Fort, House Of Wonders, The Old Cathedral, Ras Mkumbuu, Red Colobus Monkeys within the compound of Jozani Chwaka Bay National Park and much more. Take your time to visit these wonderful and stunning sites in the world.

Ngezi Forest Reserve

Pemba

Ngezi Forest Reserve is a forest reserve located in Pemba Island, Tanzania. It covers an area of 1,440 hectares (3,600 acres), mostly comprising primary forest. The reserve was established in 1959.

Jozani Chwaka Bay National Park

Pemba

Jozani Chwaka Bay National Park is a 50 km 2 (19 sq mi) national park in Tanzania located on the island of Zanzibar. It is the only national park in Zanzibar.A park with amazing and stunning environments such as Mangrove and beautiful Red Colobus to make your trip and tour very awesome.

Stone Town & Darajani Market

Unguja

Stone Town also known as "Mji Mkongwe" in Swahili language is an example of Old Town that brought together elements of cultures of Africa, the Arab region, India, and Europe over centuries. Are you Looking for stunning old building built from 18th and 19th centuries, from Old Fort, house of wonder, the Jamat Khan, to the Royal Cemetry. Then this is where your destination should be!
